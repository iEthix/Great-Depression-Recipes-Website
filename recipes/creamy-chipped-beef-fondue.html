Creamy Chipped Beef Fondue (Great Depression Edition)

Creamy Chipped Beef Fondue is a classic Great Depression recipe that features thin slices of dried beef in a smooth, creamy sauce made from a roux of butter, flour, and milk, often enriched with grated cheese.

It's a simple, economical dish that is traditionally served over toast or potatoes, embodying the resourcefulness of that era.

Ingredients:

  • 1-1/3 to 1-1/2 cups milk
  • 2 packages (8 ounces each) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 package (2-1/2 ounces) thinly sliced dried beef, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 2 teaspoons ground mustard
  • 1 loaf (1 pound) French bread, cubed

Recipe Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, heat milk and cream cheese over medium heat; stir until smooth.
  2. Stir in beef, onions and mustard; heat through.
  3. Transfer to a fondue pot or 1-1/2-qt.
  4. Slow cooker; keep warm.
  5. Serve with bread cubes.
